Plain types feature smooth surfaces for straightforward applications, while serrated designs increase turbulence, enhancing heat transfer efficiency. Perforated heat exchangers incorporate holes for improved fluid mixing and reduced pressure drop. Other designs may include customized features tailored to specific industrial needs. These variations enable manufacturers to optimize heat exchanger performance for diverse applications in sectors such as HVAC, automotive, and process industries.</p></p>
